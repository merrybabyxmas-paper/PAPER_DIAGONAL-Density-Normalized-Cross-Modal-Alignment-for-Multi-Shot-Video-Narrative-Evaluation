\begin{abstract}
Text-to-multi-shot video (T2MSV) generation models are evaluated primarily on visual quality and frame-level consistency, yet these metrics fail to capture whether models can execute \emph{narrative dynamics}---the structured entry and exit of entities across shots.
We formalize multi-shot narratives as \emph{state transition matrices} $\Delta S \in \{-1,0,+1\}^{(T-1) \times N}$, where each element encodes an atomic operation: entry ($+1$), exit ($-1$), or hold ($0$).
We prove that all possible 3-shot narrative topologies can be spanned by just two basis matrices under column permutation and linear combination (\textbf{Theorem~1}), establishing mathematical completeness.
Building on this, we introduce \diagonal{}, a benchmark comprising 1,000 prompts spanning 7 narrative patterns (3 basis, 4 derived) with complementary metrics: Narrative Alignment Score, Diagonal Dominance, State Transition Accuracy, Entity Presence Accuracy, and SVD Entropy.
Evaluation of four SOTA T2MSV architectures reveals that \emph{no model achieves positive diagonal dominance on any pattern} ($\epsilon$-pass $= 0\%$), exposing a fundamental limitation we term \textbf{Narrative Blindness}.
We identify four systematic failure types---Context Bleeding (VIC: 60.8\%), Amnesia (VGoT: 66.7\%), Attention Reversion, and Identity Bleeding---and validate via human evaluation ($\rho = 0.80$ with STA).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%% ============================================================

Diffusion-based generative models have rapidly expanded from single-clip to text-to-multi-shot video (T2MSV) generation~\cite{ho2022video,svd,imagenvideo}.
Recent systems such as StoryDiffusion~\cite{storydiff}, EchoShot~\cite{echoshot}, VIC~\cite{vic}, and VGoT~\cite{vgot} generate temporally connected sequences of scenes, leveraging consistent self-attention, flow-matching, in-context learning, and cascaded pipelines~\cite{vbench,vbench2}.

However, existing benchmarks~\cite{vbench,vbench2,t2vcompbench,moviebench} evaluate identity consistency, visual quality, or single-clip compositionality, but none systematically test whether models can execute \emph{narrative dynamics}: the structured introduction, co-occurrence, and removal of entities across shots.
This gap is critical because real-world video narratives are fundamentally defined by \emph{who appears, when, and alongside whom}.

Consider a relay narrative: $A \!\to\! AB \!\to\! B$, where entity $A$ appears alone, both co-occur, then only $B$ remains (Fig.~\ref{fig:teaser}).
This requires: (1)~introducing $B$ while preserving $A$, (2)~maintaining co-occurrence, and (3)~removing $A$ while $B$ persists.
Current models systematically fail at (3)---the departed entity ``bleeds'' into subsequent shots (\textbf{Context Bleeding}).
Critically, Identity Consistency (IC)~\cite{shotadapter} \emph{rewards} this failure because persistent entities increase cross-shot similarity.
We term this the \textbf{consistency paradox}: IC is adversarial to narrative compliance.

We formalize entity dynamics as a \emph{state transition matrix} $\Delta S \in \{-1,0,+1\}^{2 \times 3}$ and prove that \textbf{all} 3-shot topologies are generated from two basis matrices (\textbf{Theorem~1}).
\diagonal{} contributes:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
  \item A \textbf{topological formalization} with mathematical completeness guarantee for multi-shot narrative structures (\S\ref{sec:formalization}).
  \item A \textbf{1,000-prompt benchmark} spanning 7 patterns with five decomposed metrics---NAS, DD, STA, EPA, SVD Entropy (\S\ref{sec:metrics}).
  \item A \textbf{four-type failure taxonomy} providing actionable architectural diagnostics (\S\ref{sec:failures}).
  \item The \textbf{consistency paradox}: holistic metrics are adversarial to narrative evaluation ($r = -0.34$) (\S\ref{sec:blindspot}).
  \item Evaluation of four SOTA T2MSV architectures revealing universal \textbf{Narrative Blindness} ($\epsilon$-pass $= 0\%$), validated by human evaluation ($\rho = 0.80$) (\S\ref{sec:experiments}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%% ============================================================

\paragraph{Multi-shot video generation.}
Diffusion-based video synthesis~\cite{ddpm,ldm,scoreflow} has advanced from UNet temporal attention~\cite{svd,imagenvideo,ho2022video} to DiT~\cite{dit,wan,sora} and flow-matching~\cite{flowmatching} architectures.
Multi-shot extensions address coherence via shared self-attention~\cite{storydiff}, cross-attention~\cite{videostudio}, latent initialization~\cite{mevg}, LLM-guided layouts~\cite{vgot,videodirectorgpt}, keyframe transitions~\cite{seine,dynamicrafter}, flow-matching portraits~\cite{echoshot}, IP-Adapter~\cite{ip_adapter}, in-context LoRA~\cite{vic,lora}, and multimodal storytelling~\cite{minidalle}.
These span four paradigms: CSA (StoryDiffusion~\cite{storydiff}), flow-matching (EchoShot~\cite{echoshot}), in-context DiT (VIC~\cite{vic}), and cascaded T2I+I2V (VGoT~\cite{vgot}).

\paragraph{Video generation benchmarks.}
Existing benchmarks quantify consistency via embedding similarity (Table~\ref{tab:benchmark}).
VBench~\cite{vbench,vbench2} computes frame-level CLIP~\cite{clip}/DINO~\cite{dinov2} similarities conflating subject and background; T2V-CompBench~\cite{t2vcompbench} targets single-clip compositionality.
ShotAdapter~\cite{shotadapter} isolates subject regions but produces \emph{chimera embeddings} in multi-entity shots.
MovieBench~\cite{moviebench} and LoCoT2V~\cite{locot2v} recognize multi-shot challenges but lack entity-level metrics and mathematical formalization.
FID~\cite{fid} and FVD~\cite{fvd} measure distributional fidelity but are agnostic to narrative structure.
\diagonal{} is the first to formalize multi-shot narratives via state transition matrices with completeness guarantees.

\section{The Consistency Paradox}
\label{sec:blindspot}
%% ============================================================

We demonstrate a structural flaw in existing evaluation: holistic similarity metrics systematically \emph{reward} narrative failures.

ShotAdapter's IC~\cite{shotadapter} computes $\text{IC} = \frac{1}{\binom{T}{2}} \sum_{i<j} \cos(\Phi_V(V_i), \Phi_V(V_j))$.
For any pattern with $\|\Delta S\|_F > 0$, the IC-maximizing generation has $\Delta S_{\text{gen}} = \mathbf{0}$---the static sequence ignoring all dynamics---because identical shots maximize pairwise similarity.
For the relay pattern, preserving $A$ in all shots yields IC $\approx 0.94$, while correct removal achieves IC $\approx 0.78$---a 17\% \emph{penalty} for correct behavior.

We validate across 3,049 videos: IC and STA correlate at $r = -0.34$ ($p < 10^{-5}$), strongest for exit-heavy patterns (Relay: $r = -0.41$).
VIC achieves highest IC (0.94) but \emph{lowest} STA (0.431); StoryDiffusion has highest STA (0.630) but lower IC (0.81).
This implies that IC maximization inadvertently selects for models \emph{worse} at narrative dynamics, motivating our decomposed metrics (\S\ref{sec:metrics}).

\section{Topological Formalization}
\label{sec:formalization}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{State Transition Matrix}

A multi-shot video has $T$ shots $(V_1, \ldots, V_T)$ with $N$ entities.
The \emph{presence matrix} $S \in \{0,1\}^{T \times N}$ records entity presence per shot.
The \emph{state transition matrix}:
\begin{equation}
\Delta S = S_{2:T} - S_{1:T-1} \in \mathbb{M}_{(T-1) \times N}\!\bigl(\{-1, 0, +1\}\bigr),
\label{eq:delta_s}
\end{equation}
encodes entry ($+1$), exit ($-1$), or hold ($0$) per entity per transition.
We focus on $T\!=\!3, N\!\leq\!3$, the minimal complete narrative cycle requiring both introduction and removal.

\subsection{Basis and Derived Patterns}

Fig.~\ref{fig:patterns} illustrates all seven patterns.
Three \emph{basis patterns} represent fundamental archetypes:

\textbf{Relay} ($\calB_1$: $A \!\to\! AB \!\to\! B$): $\calB_1 = \bigl[\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & +1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$.

\textbf{Split} ($\calB_2$: $AB \!\to\! A \!\to\! B$): $\calB_2 = \bigl[\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ -1 & +1 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$.

\textbf{Accumulation} ($\calB_3$: $A \!\to\! AB \!\to\! ABC$): $\calB_3 = \bigl[\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & +1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & +1 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$.

Four \emph{derived patterns} via time-reversal $\calT$ and column permutation $\calP_{ij}$:
\textbf{Reduction} $= \calT(\calB_3)$;
\textbf{Convergence} $= \calP_{AB}(\calT(\calB_2))$;
\textbf{Reverse Relay} $= \calP_{AB}(\calB_1)$;
\textbf{Sliding Window} $=$ superposition of relay and exit (4 transitions).

\subsection{Completeness Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Completeness]
Under column permutation $\calP_{ij}$ and integer linear combination, $\calB_1$ and $\calB_2$ span $\mathbb{M}_{2 \times 3}(\mathbb{Z})$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Step~1.}
$v_1 = \calB_1 = E_{12} - E_{21}$,
$v_2 = \calP_{13}(\calB_1) = E_{12} - E_{23}$,
$v_3 = \calP_{23}(\calP_{12}(\calB_1)) = E_{11} - E_{23}$.
$X = v_3 - v_2 + v_1 = E_{11} - E_{21}$.
\textbf{Step~2.}
$X + \calP_{12}(\calB_2) = -E_{22}$.
\textbf{Step~3.}
From $E_{22}$: permutations yield $E_{21}, E_{23}$; $X + E_{21} = E_{11}$; further permutations give $E_{12}, E_{13}$.
All six $\{E_{ij}\}$ obtained; any $\Delta S$ is their integer combination. \qedhere
\end{proof}

Theorem~1 guarantees \emph{mathematical completeness}: any 3-shot narrative is expressible via our basis patterns, distinguishing \diagonal{} from heuristic benchmarks~\cite{moviebench,locot2v}.

\subsection{Cross-Modal Similarity Matrix}

For 3 shots and evaluation prompts $\{T_1, T_2, T_3\}$:
\begin{equation}
\Mobs[i,j] = \cos\!\bigl(\Phi_V(V_i),\; \Phi_T(T_j)\bigr),
\label{eq:mobs}
\end{equation}
where $\Phi_V, \Phi_T$ are CLIP ViT-L/14~\cite{clip} encoders.
Each shot is represented by mean-pooled features of 5 uniformly sampled frames.

\subsection{Narrative Alignment Score (NAS)}
Row-normalized alignment:
\begin{equation}
\text{NAS} = \frac{1}{3} \sum_{i=1}^{3} \frac{\Mobs[i,i]}{\sum_{j=1}^{3} \Mobs[i,j]}.
\label{eq:nas}
\end{equation}
NAS $= 1/3$ for random alignment, $= 1$ for perfect specificity.

\subsection{Diagonal Dominance (DD)}
Whether the correct prompt achieves highest similarity:
\begin{equation}
\text{DD}_i = \Mobs[i,i] - \max_{j \neq i} \Mobs[i,j].
\label{eq:dd}
\end{equation}
The $\epsilon$-pass rate requires $\text{DD}_i > 0.02$ for all $i$.
DD captures whether shots are \emph{distinctively} matched to their prompts.

\subsection{Entity Presence Accuracy (EPA)}
Entity-specific CLIP probes: $E[i,k] = \cos(\Phi_V(V_i), \Phi_T(\text{``a photo of } e_k\text{''}))$.
Binarized via per-shot median threshold:
\begin{equation}
\text{EPA} = \frac{1}{3N}\sum_{i,k} \mathbb{1}\!\left[\hat{P}_{ik} = P^*_{ik}\right].
\label{eq:epa}
\end{equation}
EPA captures both false positives (Context Bleeding) and false negatives (Amnesia).

\subsection{State Transition Accuracy (STA)}
Dynamic transition accuracy between shots:
\begin{equation}
\text{STA} = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{A}|} \sum_{(t,e) \in \mathcal{A}} \mathbb{1}\!\left[\text{sgn}(\Delta E_{t,e}) = \text{sgn}(\Delta S_{t,e})\right],
\label{eq:sta}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{A} = \{(t,e) : \Delta S_{t,e} \neq 0\}$ and $\Delta E_{t,e} = E[t\!+\!1,e] - E[t,e]$.
STA evaluates only transition \emph{direction}, making it robust to CLIP sensitivity variations.
A model generating identical shots receives STA $= 0$ for any non-trivial pattern.

\subsection{SVD Entropy}
Per-shot semantic complexity from frame-feature SVD:
\begin{equation}
\calH(V_t) = -\sum_i \tilde{\sigma}_i \log \tilde{\sigma}_i, \quad \tilde{\sigma}_i = \sigma_i / {\textstyle\sum_j} \sigma_j.
\label{eq:svd_entropy}
\end{equation}
The \emph{trajectory correctness rate} measures whether $\Delta\calH$ matches entity count changes.

\subsection{Failure Taxonomy}
\label{sec:failures}
Four types based on discrepancies between $\Delta S$ and observations:

\textbf{Type~I: Context Bleeding} ($\Delta S = -1$, entity persists).
The departed entity's visual signature remains in subsequent shots.

\textbf{Type~II: Amnesia} ($\Delta S = +1$, entity absent).
A new entity fails to appear despite explicit instruction.

\textbf{Type~III: Attention Reversion.}
$M_{11} > M_{22} < M_{33}$: the model reverts to initial content.

\textbf{Type~IV: Identity Bleeding.}
Near-uniform similarity rows: failure to distinguish entities visually.

Basis patterns receive 200 prompts each; derived patterns 100 each (Table~\ref{tab:pattern_dist}).

\paragraph{Prompt design.}
Prompts are generated via Gemini~1.5~\cite{gemini} with structured templates specifying: narrative pattern, entities from 20 categories (humans, animals, objects), scene themes from 10 environments, and per-shot descriptions.
Following~\cite{dalle3}, each scenario has three variants: \texttt{gen\_prompt} (quality tags), \texttt{eval\_prompt} (clean for CLIP), and \texttt{diag\_prompt} (entity probes).
Entity pairs have mean CLIP text similarity 0.23 ($\sigma\!=\!0.08$); pairs with similarity $> 0.45$ are excluded.

\paragraph{Quality control.}
All prompts undergo automated validation (entity--pattern topology matching) and 10\% manual spot-checking (97\% adherence).

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%% ============================================================

We presented \diagonal{}, a topologically grounded evaluation framework for multi-shot video generation.
Our completeness theorem (Theorem~1) provides the first mathematical guarantee for a video generation benchmark.
Evaluation of four SOTA architectures reveals universal Narrative Blindness ($\epsilon$-pass $= 0\%$) with architecture-specific failure profiles: VIC suffers Context Bleeding (60.8\%) from in-context conditioning, VGoT Amnesia (66.7\%) from planning-rendering decoupling, and StoryDiffusion Identity Bleeding (21.7\%) from CSA homogenization.
The consistency paradox ($r = -0.34$ between IC and STA; $\rho = 0.80$ human validation) demonstrates that holistic metrics are adversarial to narrative evaluation.

Progress requires entity-aware conditioning: entity-specific attention masks, narrative-conditioned guidance~\cite{cfg}, or entity slot architectures that decouple identity from presence.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
\diagonal{} evaluates 3-shot / $\leq$3-entity sequences; extension to variable-length narratives is future work.
CLIP-based metrics capture coarse-grained presence; detection models~\cite{groundingdino} could improve granularity.
VIC (449/1K) and VGoT (600/1K) did not complete all scenarios.
We release benchmark, code, and all 3,049 generated videos.
