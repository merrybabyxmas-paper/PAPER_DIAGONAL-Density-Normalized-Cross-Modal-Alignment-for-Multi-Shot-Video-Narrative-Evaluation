\begin{abstract}
Text-to-multi-shot video (T2MSV) models are evaluated on visual quality and frame-level consistency, yet these metrics fail to capture \emph{narrative dynamics}---the structured entry and exit of entities across shots.
We formalize multi-shot narratives as \emph{state transition matrices} $\Delta S \in \{-1,0,+1\}^{(T-1) \times N}$ and prove that all 3-shot narrative topologies are spanned by two basis matrices under permutation and linear combination (\textbf{Theorem~1}).
Building on this, we introduce \diagonal{}, a 1,000-prompt benchmark spanning 7 patterns with complementary metrics: Narrative Alignment Score (NAS), Diagonal Dominance (DD), State Transition Accuracy (STA), Entity Presence Accuracy (EPA), and SVD Entropy.
Evaluation of four SOTA architectures reveals \emph{no model achieves positive diagonal dominance on any pattern} ($\epsilon$-pass rate $= 0\%$).
We identify four failure types---Context Bleeding (VIC: 60.8\%), Amnesia (VGoT: 66.7\%), Attention Reversion, and Identity Bleeding---and validate via human evaluation ($\rho = 0.80$ with STA).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%% ============================================================

Diffusion-based models have expanded video synthesis from single-clip to text-to-multi-shot video (T2MSV) generation.
Systems such as StoryDiffusion~\cite{storydiff}, EchoShot~\cite{echoshot}, VIC~\cite{vic}, and VGoT~\cite{vgot} generate temporally connected scene sequences, yet existing benchmarks~\cite{vbench,t2vcompbench,moviebench} evaluate only identity consistency, visual quality, or single-clip compositionality---none systematically test whether models can execute \emph{narrative dynamics}: the structured introduction, co-occurrence, and removal of entities across shots.

Consider a relay narrative $A \!\to\! AB \!\to\! B$: entity $A$ must exit while $B$ persists.
Current models systematically fail---the departed entity ``bleeds'' into subsequent shots (\textbf{Context Bleeding}).
Critically, holistic metrics like Identity Consistency (IC)~\cite{shotadapter} \emph{reward} this failure because persistent entities \emph{increase} cross-shot similarity.
We term this the \textbf{consistency paradox}: IC is adversarial to narrative compliance for any non-trivial pattern (Fig.~\ref{fig:teaser}).

We formalize entity dynamics as a \emph{state transition matrix} $\Delta S \in \{-1,0,+1\}^{2 \times 3}$ and prove that \textbf{all} 3-shot narrative topologies are generated from two basis matrices (Theorem~1).
Building on this, \diagonal{} contributes:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
  \item A topological formalization of multi-shot narratives with a completeness guarantee (\S\ref{sec:formalization}).
  \item A \textbf{1,000-prompt benchmark} (7 patterns) with decomposed metrics: NAS, DD, STA, EPA, SVD Entropy (\S\ref{sec:metrics}).
  \item Evaluation of four SOTA T2MSV models revealing universal \textbf{Narrative Blindness} (DD $<0$, $\epsilon$-pass $=0\%$), a four-type failure taxonomy, and human evaluation validation (\S\ref{sec:experiments}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%% ============================================================

\paragraph{Multi-shot video generation.}
Video synthesis has advanced from UNet temporal attention~\cite{svd,imagenvideo} through cascaded pipelines~\cite{ho2022video} to DiT~\cite{dit}.
Multi-shot extensions use shared self-attention~\cite{storydiff}, cross-attention~\cite{videostudio}, latent initialization~\cite{mevg}, LLM-guided layouts~\cite{vgot}, flow-matching~\cite{echoshot}, and in-context LoRA~\cite{vic}.

\paragraph{Benchmarks.}
VBench 2.0~\cite{vbench} conflates subject and background similarity; T2V-CompBench~\cite{t2vcompbench} targets single-clip compositionality.
ShotAdapter~\cite{shotadapter} uses IC designed for single-entity shots---multi-entity IC produces \emph{chimera embeddings} that mask entity-removal failures.
MovieBench~\cite{moviebench} and LoCoT2V-Bench~\cite{locot2v} lack entity-level metrics and mathematical formalization.
\diagonal{} is the first to formalize narratives via state transition matrices with completeness guarantees (Table~\ref{tab:benchmark}).

\section{Topological Formalization}
\label{sec:formalization}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{State Transition Matrix}

Entity evolution across $T$ shots with $N$ entities is encoded as:
\begin{equation}
\Delta S \in \mathbb{M}_{(T-1) \times N}\!\bigl(\{-1, 0, +1\}\bigr),
\label{eq:delta_s}
\end{equation}
where $\Delta S_{t,e}$ records entity $e$'s change from shot $t$ to $t\!+\!1$: entry ($+1$), exit ($-1$), or hold ($0$).
We focus on $T\!=\!3, N\!\leq\!3$, yielding a $2 \times 3$ matrix.

\subsection{Basis and Derived Patterns}

Three \emph{basis patterns} represent fundamental archetypes:

\noindent\textbf{Relay} ($\calB_1$: $A \!\to\! AB \!\to\! B$):
$\calB_1 = \bigl[\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & +1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$.
\textbf{Split} ($\calB_2$: $AB \!\to\! A \!\to\! B$):
$\calB_2 = \bigl[\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ -1 & +1 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$.
\textbf{Accumulation} ($\calB_3$: $A \!\to\! AB \!\to\! ABC$):
$\calB_3 = \bigl[\begin{smallmatrix} 0 & +1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & +1 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$.

Four \emph{derived patterns} are obtained via time-reversal $\calT$ (negate and reverse rows) and column permutation $\calP_{ij}$:
Reduction $= \calT(\calB_3)$,
Convergence $= \calP_{AB}(\calT(\calB_2))$,
Reverse Relay $= \calP_{AB}(\calB_1)$, and
Sliding Window (superposition of relay and exit).

\subsection{Completeness Theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Under column permutation and integer-coefficient linear combination, $\calB_1$ and $\calB_2$ span $\mathbb{M}_{2 \times 3}(\mathbb{Z})$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
\textbf{Step~1:} Three permutation variants of $\calB_1$ yield $X = E_{11} - E_{21}$ via cancellation.
\textbf{Step~2:} $X + \calP_{12}(\calB_2) = -E_{22}$, isolating a single basis element.
\textbf{Step~3:} Column permutations propagate to all six standard basis matrices $\{E_{ij}\}$.
Any $\Delta S \in \mathbb{M}_{2 \times 3}(\mathbb{Z})$ is a unique integer combination of these. Full proof in Supplementary~A.
\end{proof}

\textbf{Implication.}
Our 7 patterns cover all cinematically relevant topologies; the theorem guarantees no fundamentally new pattern exists outside this algebraic span.

\section{The DIAGONAL Framework}
\label{sec:metrics}
%% ============================================================

Given a 3-shot video with evaluation prompts $\{T_1, T_2, T_3\}$, we compute:

\paragraph{Cross-modal similarity matrix.}
$\Mobs[i,j] = \cos(\Phi_V(V_i), \Phi_T(T_j))$ using CLIP ViT-L/14, with 5 mean-pooled frames per shot.

\paragraph{Narrative Alignment Score (NAS).}
Row-normalized alignment: $\text{NAS} = \frac{1}{3}\sum_i \Mobs[i,i] / \sum_j \Mobs[i,j]$.

\paragraph{Diagonal Dominance (DD).}
$\text{DD}_i = \Mobs[i,i] - \max_{j \neq i} \Mobs[i,j]$;
$\epsilon$-pass requires DD$_i > 0.02$ for all $i$.

\paragraph{Entity Presence Accuracy (EPA).}
Per-entity CLIP probes $E[i,k] = \cos(\Phi_V(V_i), \Phi_T(\text{``a } e_k\text{''}))$, binarized via per-shot median. $\text{EPA} = \frac{1}{3N}\sum_{i,k} \mathbb{1}[\hat{P}_{ik} = P^*_{ik}]$.

\paragraph{State Transition Accuracy (STA).}
Fraction of non-zero $\Delta S$ entries where $\text{sgn}(\Delta E_{t,e}) = \text{sgn}(\Delta S_{t,e})$.

\paragraph{SVD Entropy.}
Per-shot semantic complexity: $\calH(V_t) = -\sum_i \tilde{\sigma}_i \log \tilde{\sigma}_i$ from frame-feature SVD.
Trajectory correctness measures whether $\Delta\calH$ matches entity count changes.

\paragraph{Failure taxonomy.}
Four types based on $\Delta S$ vs.\ observed changes:
\textbf{Type~I} (Context Bleeding): failed exit ($\Delta S\!=\!-1$, entity persists).
\textbf{Type~II} (Amnesia): failed entry ($\Delta S\!=\!+1$, entity absent).
\textbf{Type~III} (Attention Reversion): U-shaped diagonal ($M_{11} > M_{22} < M_{33}$).
\textbf{Type~IV} (Identity Bleeding): near-uniform similarity rows.

\section{Dataset Construction}
\label{sec:dataset}
%% ============================================================

We construct 1,000 scenarios (3,000 shots) across 7 patterns (Table~\ref{tab:pattern_dist}).
Prompts are generated via Google Gemini with structured templates specifying pattern, entities (20 categories: human, animal, professional), and scene themes (10 environments).
Each prompt has three variants: \texttt{gen\_prompt} (with quality tags), \texttt{eval\_prompt} (clean description for CLIP), and \texttt{diag\_prompt} (entity probe).
Entity pairs are selected for visual distinguishability (mean CLIP text similarity: 0.23).

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{Setup}

We evaluate four T2MSV architectures:
\textbf{StoryDiffusion}~\cite{storydiff} (CSA over SDXL + SEINE I2V, 30+50 steps),
\textbf{EchoShot}~\cite{echoshot} (flow-matching, Wan2.1-1.3B, 50 steps),
\textbf{VIC}~\cite{vic} (CogVideoX-5B + LoRA, 50 steps), and
\textbf{VGoT}~\cite{vgot} (Kolors T2I + DynamiCrafter I2V, 25+50 steps).
All use seed = 42.
VIC completed 449/1000 and VGoT 600/1000 scenarios.
Evaluation uses CLIP ViT-L/14 on 4 RTX 4090 GPUs.

Table~\ref{tab:failures} quantifies architecture-specific vulnerabilities:
\textbf{VIC's Context Bleeding (60.8\%)} reflects the tension between context preservation (desirable for consistency) and suppression (required for narrative dynamics).

Types I and II frequently co-occur (31.2\%), indicating systemic inability to manage entity transitions rather than isolated failures.
Types III and IV show low mutual co-occurrence (2.1\%), representing distinct architectural deficiencies.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%% ============================================================

We presented \diagonal{}, a topologically grounded evaluation framework that formalizes narrative dynamics as state transition matrices.
Theorem~1 provides the first mathematical completeness guarantee for a video generation benchmark.
Evaluation reveals universal \textbf{Narrative Blindness}: no model achieves DD $> 0$, and 77--91\% of videos exhibit at least one systematic failure.
The consistency paradox---holistic metrics rewarding narrative failures---is confirmed both analytically and via human evaluation ($\rho = 0.80$).
Progress requires entity-aware conditioning that independently manages entity introduction, persistence, and removal.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
\diagonal{} evaluates 3-shot, $\leq$3-entity sequences; extension to variable-length narratives is future work.
CLIP-based metrics capture coarse-grained entity presence; combining with detection-based methods could improve granularity.
VIC (449/1000) and VGoT (600/1000) did not complete all scenarios.
